◆ Part 01 — Radial value map and movable hierarchy ◆
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
What is simulated — Each market object receives a point in a circular field: goodness on one axis, popularity on the other, hierarchy as a height-like layer above it.
Why this is simulated — The model must separate what is liked from what is good; otherwise a harmful but attractive object looks the same as a useful object.

Abbreviations used only in this part
  GV = Goodness value: positive values mean social benefit, fewer harms, fairer side effects; negative values mean damage or exploitation.
  PV = Popularity value: public demand, attraction, fashion, habit or approval; it is deliberately not identical to goodness.
  HV = Hierarchy value: structural rank; high values mean many lower actions, roles or consequences depend on the object.
  AD = Axis direction: the angle created by goodness and popularity; it reveals the quadrant of the object.
  RW = Radial width: distance from the neutral center; high values mean a very clear or polarizing position.
  MW = Moral weight: compressed comparison signal from goodness, popularity, hierarchy and risk.
Units used only in this part
  pts = points on a normalized scale
  deg = angle degrees of the circular direction
  MCU = moral currency units

◆ Part 02 — Institution, population and knowledge signals ◆
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
What is simulated — Selected products are repeatedly re-scored. Institutions push goodness, population shifts popularity, and knowledge corrects both when measured consequences appear.
Why this is simulated — A moral market layer is fragile if one actor can define truth alone. This step tests whether separated signals produce a more stable value vector.

Abbreviations used only in this part
  IS = Institutional signal: law, audit and public authority acting on harm, benefit and fairness.
  PS = Population signal: collective desire, dislike, fashion, habit and public attention.
  KS = Knowledge signal: measured consequences, research and evidence correcting both sides.
  GV = Goodness value after signal pressure.
  PV = Popularity value after signal pressure.
  DG = Delta goodness: the change of goodness in the current step.
  DP = Delta popularity: the change of popularity in the current step.
Units used only in this part
  pts = points on the value scale
  % = signal strength in percent
  day = simulated decision day

◆ Part 03 — Product market with moral price environment ◆
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
What is simulated — Products are sold through demand, supply, tax or relief and moral weight. The price is still numeric, but it is embedded in a colored value context.
Why this is simulated — A market price can hide exploitation, addiction or external costs. The simulation makes the hidden context visible before transactions are counted.

Abbreviations used only in this part
  RP = Reference price: end price after popularity, goodness, hierarchy and tax-or-relief pressure.
  DI = Demand index: current pull of customers toward the product.
  SU = Supply units: available product pieces in this step.
  TR = Tax-or-relief rate: positive means surcharge, negative means support.
  TX = Transactions: simulated completed product exchanges.
  GV = Goodness value used by the filter.
  PV = Popularity value used by demand.
  MW = Moral weight used as context, not as a bare price.
Units used only in this part
  EUR = conventional price reference
  % = rate, index or share
  pc = counted product pieces
  tx = counted transactions
  MCU = moral currency units

◆ Part 04 — Labor market: workers choose employers too ◆
══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
What is simulated — Workers and employers are matched by productive ability, selfless contribution, fairness and moral weight. The employer does not monopolize judgment.
Why this is simulated — Classic labor markets often overrate pay and underrate employer quality. This part tests a more symmetric market relation.

Abbreviations used only in this part
  WP = Worker potential: skill, reliability and productive capacity of the worker.
  SE = Selflessness: contribution beyond narrow private gain.
  FR = Fairness rate: employer-side fairness from wages, risk and goodness.
  MG = Match grade: quality of the worker-employer fit.
  WA = Wage adjustment: change in pay caused by match and fairness.
  MW = Moral weight of worker and employer context.
Units used only in this part
  pts = points for skill, fairness and fit
  % = rate or probability share
  EUR = monthly wage reference
  pers = number of people
  MCU = moral currency units
