Truth-Cuboid Economy — Version 2

Core correction

This model starts again from the corrected idea. The truth value is not a later legal challenge, not an automatic appeal mechanism, and not mainly a tool for invalidating a trade after the fact. The truth value is itself part of the trade.

A transaction does not consist only of a number such as 100. It consists of at least two equal coordinates:

Q=(z,τ) Q=(z,\tau)

  • zz is the numerical coordinate: amount, price, claim, debt, wage, cost, asset value.
  • τ\tau is the truth coordinate: how real, genuine, actual, correct, known, verified, or falsified something is.
  • τ\tau lies between −1-1 and +1+1.
  • Both parties may agree directly on zz and τ\tau.
  • Or both parties may agree only on a volume VV plus two signs: one sign for the number and one sign for truth.

Truth is therefore not merely an evaluation of trade. It is a tradable substance. Knowledge, education, insight, proof, verification, and falsification become economic goods.

1. The unit: number and truth as two dimensions of trade

A normal market trades objects against numbers. This system trades objects against number-truth pairs.

A traded unit is:

Q=(a,σz,b,στ,h) Q=(a,\sigma_z,b,\sigma_\tau,h)

where:

  • a≥0a \geq 0: amount or numerical strength.
  • b∈[0,1]b \in [0,1]: truth strength.
  • σz∈{−1,+1}\sigma_z \in \{-1,+1\}: sign of the number.
  • στ∈{−1,+1}\sigma_\tau \in \{-1,+1\}: sign of the truth coordinate.
  • hh: stack height, meaning quantity, time, repetition, weight, or layer.

The actual number is:

z=σz⋅a z=\sigma_z \cdot a

The actual truth value is:

τ=στ⋅b \tau=\sigma_\tau \cdot b

The volume is:

V=z⋅τ⋅h V=z \cdot \tau \cdot h

or:

V=σzστabh V=\sigma_z \sigma_\tau a b h

The essential point is that VV alone is not enough information. The two signs must be stored separately. A positive volume can arise from:

(+z)(+τ) (+z)(+\tau)

or from:

(−z)(−τ) (-z)(-\tau)

A negative volume can arise from:

(+z)(−τ) (+z)(-\tau)

or from:

(−z)(+τ) (-z)(+\tau)

This origin of the signs is the fourth information.

2. The four quadrants

Number Truth Volume Economic meaning
++ ++ ++ positive number with positive truth: creation of real value
++ −- −- positive number with negative truth: trade in falsehood, fiction, error, or counter-reality
−- ++ −- negative number with positive truth: surrender, cost, removal, real negation
−- −- ++ negative number with negative truth: removal of falsehood, falsification, correction of error

A negative truth value does not automatically mean “illegal” or “morally bad”. It means that the traded assertion, property, or claim has a negative relation to reality.

In knowledge markets this can be extremely valuable. Showing that a hypothesis is false is useful economic knowledge.

3. Two ways to agree on a trade

Mode A: agreement on number and truth value

The parties say:

z=100,τ=0.8 z=100,\quad \tau=0.8

The volume is:

V=80 V=80

Example: A course is sold for 100 units and both sides agree that the course has a truth or insight coordinate of 0.80.8. This does not mean nobody can ever be wrong. It means that within the system this course is traded as 100 numerical units with a truth coordinate of 0.8.

Mode B: agreement on volume and signs

The parties say:

V=80,σz=+,στ=+ V=80,\quad \sigma_z=+,\quad \sigma_\tau=+

They now know that the trade concerns positive numerical volume with positive truth. The exact decomposition may be specified by the contract or by market rules:

100⋅0.8=80 100 \cdot 0.8 = 80

or

80⋅1=80 80 \cdot 1 = 80

or

200⋅0.4=80 200 \cdot 0.4 = 80

These forms have the same volume but different economic meaning. Therefore a market can trade either the pair (z,τ)(z,\tau) or the volume with a sign pair and a decomposition rule.

4. What “truth” means here

Truth here does not mean absolute divine truth. It is an economic coordinate for closeness to reality.

τ=+1 \tau=+1

means: fully real, accurate, proven, functional, confirmed in practice.

τ=0 \tau=0

means: neutral, unknown, not yet classified, undecided.

τ=−1 \tau=-1

means: refuted, false, counterfactual, non-real with respect to the traded claim.

Truth is therefore not simply “good” or “bad”. It is a direction on an axis of knowledge.

A fairy tale, for example, can have positive value as a physical book or cultural work, while having neutral or negative truth as a factual claim about the world. The market must therefore always specify what the truth coordinate refers to.

5. The reference object of truth

Every truth value needs a reference object:

R=(object,claim,context) R=(object, claim, context)

Examples:

  • “This bread weighs 1 kg.”
  • “This machine operates at 80 % efficiency.”
  • “This theory explains the data.”
  • “This course teaches correct mathematics.”
  • “This diagnosis describes the patient’s condition.”
  • “This hypothesis is false.”
  • “This certificate confirms the origin.”

The traded unit is then:

Q=(z,τ,R) Q=(z,\tau,R)

Without a reference object, the truth value is unusable. One must know what the truth is about.

6. Knowledge as a commodity

Knowledge in this system is not merely text. It is a truth cuboid.

A knowledge good consists of claims:

W={r1,r2,…,rn} W=\{r_1,r_2,\ldots,r_n\}

Each claim has:

Qi=(zi,τi) Q_i=(z_i,\tau_i)

The total value of the knowledge is:

V(W)=∑iziτi V(W)=\sum_i z_i\tau_i

A textbook, database, scientific report, formula, blueprint, or market analysis can be traded this way.

Example: A report costs 1000. It contains 10 important claims. Eight are strongly confirmed; two are speculative.

τtotal=0.72 \tau_{total}=0.72

Its knowledge volume is:

V=1000⋅0.72=720 V=1000 \cdot 0.72=720

This does not mean the report costs only 720. It means the report carries 1000 numerical units and 720 units of truth volume.

7. Education as trade in truth capability

Education is more than information. It changes the ability of a person or system to recognize, use, and generate truth.

An education cuboid is:

B=(z,ΔK,τ) B=(z,\Delta K,\tau)

  • zz: price or effort of the education.
  • ΔK\Delta K: increase in skill, understanding, orientation, or competence.
  • τ\tau: truth value of the transmitted contents and methods.

A bad course can be expensive and still have low or negative truth. A good course increases the learner’s capacity for insight.

Education therefore trades not only content but truth capacity.

Examples:

  • Mathematics instruction: positive truth and high transferability.
  • Propaganda: positive number, negative relation to truth.
  • Critical-thinking training: positive value because it improves future verification and falsification.
  • Pseudoteaching: may exist as a market good, but with negative truth coordinate.

8. Verification as a commodity

Verification is trade in positive confirmation.

A verification performs:

R→τR↑ R \rightarrow \tau_R \uparrow

It does not necessarily increase the nominal number zz; it increases the truth coordinate τ\tau.

Example:

A company owns a technical claim:

R=“The engine reaches 90 % efficiency.” R=\text{“The engine reaches 90 % efficiency.”}

Before testing:

Q=(10000,0.3) Q=(10000,0.3)

After strong verification:

Q=(10000,0.85) Q=(10000,0.85)

The volume rises from:

3000 3000

to:

8500 8500

The verification itself is a tradable service. The verifier does not merely sell a document; the verifier sells an increase in market-recognized reality alignment.

9. Falsification as a commodity

Falsification is not merely destruction. It is often more valuable than confirmation because it ends false paths.

There are two representations.

Representation 1: negative truth of a claim

The claim is:

R=“Treatment X cures disease Y.” R=\text{“Treatment X cures disease Y.”}

If the claim is false:

τR=−0.9 \tau_R=-0.9

A report can be sold that demonstrates this negative truth coordinate.

Representation 2: positive truth of the counterclaim

The counterclaim is:

R′=“Treatment X does not cure disease Y.” R'=\text{“Treatment X does not cure disease Y.”}

If that counterclaim is correct:

τR′=+0.9 \tau_{R'}=+0.9

Both forms are possible, but they must be distinguished.

Economically, falsification is valuable because it prevents false investments, false education, false technology, and false policy.

It contains at least four sums:

S++,S+−,S−+,S−− S_{++}, S_{+-}, S_{-+}, S_{--}

From these follow:

But the quadrant sums remain visible. An account with positive volume can consist of built truth or removed falsehood. Both can be valuable, but they are not the same.

12. Currency: national number and planetary truth

The currency has two layers:

  1. National coordinate: local number, prices, wages, purchasing power.
  2. Planetary coordinate: common unit of account for number-truth volume.

A country may have local prices, but the planetary calculation compares real volumes.

P=zLcL⋅τ P=\frac{z_L}{c_L}\cdot \tau

  • zLz_L: local number.
  • cLc_L: national coefficient.
  • τ\tau: truth value.
  • PP: planetary truth-value.

The comparison is not only “How expensive?” but “How much real, true, functional, recognized substance is being traded?”

23. Conclusion

The truth-cuboid economy is a market model in which number and truth are traded together. Truth is not a later judgment about a transaction. It is an independent coordinate inside the transaction itself.

This allows knowledge, education, insight, verification, and falsification to become normal economic goods. A positive truth value sells confirmed reality. A negative truth value sells the knowledge that a claim does not match reality. Both can be valuable.

The fourth information remains necessary because the same volume can arise from different sign pairs. Only by storing the numerical sign and the truth sign separately can the market distinguish building real value, trading falsehood, representing real negation, and removing error.
