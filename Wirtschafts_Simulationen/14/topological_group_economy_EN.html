Topological Group Economy (TGE)

A Complete Single-Currency Economic System for Countries, Groups, Planets, and Multiple Galaxies

Currency: Solution Number (SN)
Mathematical primitives: Topology + algebraic group
Goal: Problem-solving becomes economically positive. Problem-creation becomes economically negative.

0. Summary

The Topological Group Economy, abbreviated TGE, is an economic system with exactly one currency: the Solution Number (SN).

An SN is not a normal coin and not merely a database entry. An SN is the numerical value of a stack made from:

  1. a topological context,
  2. a group value,
  3. an economic history,
  4. an effect of problem-solving or problem-creation.

The core idea is:

Topology + algebraic group → stack → number → currency

Positive SN arises when problems are solved, reduced, stabilized, or repaired.

Negative SN arises when problems are created, worsened, externalized, hidden, or fraudulently misrepresented.

The system is not magical. It does not replace physics, politics, courts, or ethics. But it makes one radical economic move:

Whoever solves problems becomes positive.
Whoever creates problems becomes negative.
Whoever does both is accounted for honestly.

1. Mathematical Core

The system uses only two primitive structures:

  1. an algebraic group,
  2. a topology.

Everything else is built from these as an application, contract, institution, or protocol.

1.1 The Algebraic Group

The currency is based on the additive group of real numbers:

G=(ℝ,+,0,−) G = (\mathbb{R}, +, 0, -)

This means:

  • There is addition.
  • There is a neutral element 00.
  • Every value has a negative inverse.
  • Account balances can be positive or negative.

Examples:

+1000 SN +1000 \text{ SN}

means: the actor has net problem-solving value, credit, or claim.

−1000 SN -1000 \text{ SN}

means: the actor has net problem-creation, debt, open obligation, or repair burden.

The group provides calculability.

1.2 The Topology

There is a problem space:

X X

On this problem space there is a topology:

τ \tau

An open set:

U∈τ U \in \tau

represents a context in which problems, assets, or solutions belong together.

Examples of topological domains:

  • water infrastructure for small cities,
  • energy supply for cold planets,
  • the labor market of a country,
  • conflict resolution between states,
  • galactic transport logistics,
  • radiation shielding for outposts,
  • credit default risk of an industry,
  • food supply under resource scarcity.

The topology does not directly say how much something is worth. The group does that.

The topology says:

  • Which problems are similar.
  • Which solutions are transferable.
  • Which assets are compatible.
  • Which markets belong together.
  • Which organizations, countries, planets, or galaxies are economically connectable.
  • Where fusion is possible.
  • Where adapter work, bridges, or translation are required.

The topology provides compatibility.

In this topological context, a specific positive or negative group value was created, transferred, or corrected.

The numerical value of the stack is:

Z(S)=g1+g2+…+gn Z(S)=g_1+g_2+\dots+g_n

The application domain of the stack is:

D(S)=U1∩U2∩…∩Un D(S)=U_1 \cap U_2 \cap \dots \cap U_n

D(S)≠⌀ D(S)\neq \varnothing

If:

D(S)=⌀ D(S)=\varnothing

Central principle:

The group creates the value.
The topology decides whether things fit together.

2. Fusion, Combination, and Bundling

Two stacks can be combined:

Z(S⊕T)=Z(S)+Z(T) Z(S \oplus T)=Z(S)+Z(T)

  • Contexts must overlap.

An engineer would say:

Two modules can only be combined if their interfaces are compatible.

In the TGE, topology is the interface logic.

People are not “problem people.” Concrete effects are evaluated, not a person’s existence.

11.5 War in the System

War almost always creates massive negative stacks.

Not only because of direct destruction, but because of:

  • injuries,

  • loss of trust,

  • future risks,

Loot may be a positive asset transfer, but the negative stacks caused by the attack are counted against it.

Aggressive war is usually massively negative.

Defense can be positive or neutral if it prevents greater damage. But defense also creates costs and damage that must be accounted for honestly.

Goal:

Escalation becomes expensive.
Repair becomes valuable.
Stable cooperation becomes profitable.

17.1 Basic Rule Against Problem Farming

No one may profit by creating a problem and then selling its solution.

Rule:

self-caused damage>possible repair profit \text{self-caused damage} > \text{possible repair profit}

If A causes 1000 SN of damage and later repairs it for 800 SN, A remains at:

plus a penalty, liability, or trust-loss stack.
