# If the BRICS states were governed directly democratically like Switzerland

Yes — but it would **not simply lead to “more democracy.”** It would either **break BRICS apart** or transform it into something entirely different: a **direct-democratic mega-confederation**.

The first hard point: BRICS is not a state today, but a political-diplomatic coordination forum. The official BRICS description lists eleven members and describes the bloc as a forum for cooperation; decisions there run through consensus among states, not through the will of citizens. Source: [BRICS — About the BRICS](https://brics.br/en/about-the-brics). Switzerland, by contrast, allows citizens to correct parliamentary decisions by referendum and to propose constitutional amendments by popular initiative; Swiss federal sources describe initiatives and referendums as the two central instruments of direct democracy. Source: [About Switzerland — Direct democracy](https://aboutswitzerland.eda.admin.ch/en/direct-democracy).

So the transformation would not be:

```text
BRICS + votes
```

but:

```text
BRICS states lose their special diplomatic status
→ citizens become sovereign
→ states become cantons / republics / demoi
→ BRICS becomes a super-federal state
```

## 1. It would first destroy the core of BRICS

BRICS currently survives because very different regimes can negotiate with one another:

```text
China        = centralized party-state
Russia       = personalized vertical of power
Iran         = theocratic-republican security order
India        = mass democracy with centralizing tendencies
Brazil       = presidential democracy
South Africa = parliamentary-presidential democracy
Gulf states  = monarchic-technocratic rentier states
```

Swiss-style direct democracy would attack precisely this governing logic.

Direct democracy needs at least:

```text
free debate
free opposition
free media
free signature collection
fair vote administration
judicial control
protection from state intimidation
```

Without that, it would not be Swiss direct democracy, but only **plebiscitary acclamation**:

```text
the regime asks the question
the regime controls the media
the regime counts the votes
the regime calls the result “the will of the people”
```

That would not be a 7-democracy. It would be a **5-central state with a voting façade**.

## 2. If it is made genuinely Swiss, BRICS turns against its strongest regimes

Real initiatives and referendums in China, Russia, Iran, Saudi Arabia, or the Emirates would be system-changing. Citizens could vote not only on taxes or infrastructure, but indirectly on the foundations of power:

```text
censorship
party monopoly
war policy
intelligence-service power
corruption
subsidies
military spending
religious law
capital movement
labor migration
environmental destruction
```

That would mean:

> A genuine BRICS direct democracy would not be a reform of BRICS.  
> It would be a democratic revolution inside several BRICS states at the same time.

Therefore the realistic first consequence would be:

```text
authoritarian members would block it,
leave,
manipulate the procedure,
or try to turn it into controlled referendums.
```

## 3. Without a cantonal majority, China and India would dominate everything

Switzerland does not rely only on the popular majority. Constitutional amendments also require a majority of the cantons; joining a supranational community likewise requires a double majority. Source: [ch.ch — Popular majority and majority of the cantons](https://www.ch.ch/en/votes-and-elections/votes/popular-majority-and-majority-of-the-cantons/).

That is decisive.

Transfer this to BRICS: India and China had roughly 1.45 and 1.41 billion inhabitants respectively according to World Bank data for 2024. Source: [World Bank Data — China and India](https://data.worldbank.org/?locations=CN-IN). Without a states' majority, India and China together could practically dominate every BRICS-wide popular vote.

Then the system would not be direct-democratic and federal, but demographically imperial:

```text
India + China
      ↓
Rest of BRICS
```

That would be politically explosive. Brazil, Russia, South Africa, Iran, Egypt, Ethiopia, Indonesia, Saudi Arabia, and the Emirates would never accept their sovereignty being overridden by Indo-Chinese population majorities.

So a BRICS-Switzerland would need at least:

```text
popular majority
+
states' majority
+
macro-regional majority
+
fundamental-rights review
```

Not:

```text
4 billion votes → simple majority
```

but:

```text
citizens' majority
+ majority of member states
+ majority of world regions / demoi
+ constitutional court
```

Only then does it become 7.

## 4. It would transform BRICS from a bloc of states into a demoicracy

The right term would not be “democracy” in the simple sense, but **demoicracy**:

```text
not one people,
but many peoples,
governing together,
without merging into a single people.
```

It would be closer to:

```text
Switzerland
+ EU
+ India
+ direct democracy
+ strong constitutional courts
+ multilingual power-sharing
```

The architecture would have to look like this:

```text
Citizens' chamber:
proportional to population

States' chamber:
each BRICS country with strong weight

Demoi chamber:
languages, minorities, indigenous groups, religious spaces

Direct democracy:
initiatives and referendums, but with distributed signature thresholds

Constitutional court:
reviews fundamental rights, minority protection, competence questions

Seven- or eleven-member directorate:
no single leader, no super-presidency
```

Otherwise direct democracy immediately becomes a machine of the largest.

## 5. The positive consequences would be enormous

If it really succeeded, something historically new would emerge.

### First: demilitarization of BRICS politics

Governments love geopolitical symbolism:

```text
anti-Western rhetoric
summit diplomacy
currency projects
military cooperation
strategic axes
```

Citizens often vote more soberly:

```text
cost of living
jobs
corruption
health
housing
education
security
infrastructure
environment
```

BRICS would function less as an anti-Western power bloc and more as a **social-economic development union**.

### Second: downward pressure on corruption

Direct democracy can disrupt elite cartels. Not automatically, but structurally.

If citizens could trigger referendums against megaprojects, treaties, subsidies, or special rights, pressure would arise against:

```text
oligarchic privileges
state-owned corporations without oversight
resource rents
megaprojects without local consent
secret foreign treaties
```

### Third: new global legitimacy

A direct-democratic BRICS would no longer be merely:

```text
“Global South against the West”
```

but:

```text
“several billion citizens vote for themselves”
```

That would have enormous symbolic power. It would put Western democracies under pressure, because BRICS could then argue not only from size, but from legitimacy.

## 6. The negative consequences would also be brutal

### First: permanent blockage

Swiss direct democracy works, among other reasons, because the state is relatively small, wealthy, institutionally stable, and federally well-practiced. In Switzerland, votes on issues are held about four times per year; average voter turnout in recent decades has been a little above 40%. Source: [About Switzerland — Direct democracy](https://aboutswitzerland.eda.admin.ch/en/direct-democracy).

At BRICS scale, this could turn into:

```text
constant voting campaigns
permanent disinformation
translation chaos
national mobilization
religious polarization
foreign-policy sabotage
```

### Second: popular votes as geopolitical weapons

Every large state could try to influence votes inside other members:

```text
China influences diaspora and platform spaces
Russia influences security narratives
India influences demographic majorities
Gulf states influence through money
Iran influences religious networks
Western actors influence counter-campaigns
```

At this size, direct democracy would be immediately manipulable without extreme transparency rules.

### Third: minority fear

Simple direct democracy can become dangerous for minorities.

That is why Switzerland needs not only a popular majority, but also a cantonal majority for constitutional questions. In BRICS, one would have to go even further:

```text
no fundamental rights removable by simple majority
no language displaceable by majority vote
no religion disenfranchised by majority vote
no region expropriated by majority vote
no state autonomy abolished by simple majority
```

Without that, direct democracy becomes a weapon of the majority.

## 7. As an angle model

Today BRICS is roughly:

```text
state ↔ state ↔ state
       consensus of governments
```

So it is closer to **6 confederation**, but with many authoritarian interior spaces.

A Swiss-style direct-democratic BRICS Union would turn this into:

```text
citizen ↔ state ↔ region ↔ demos ↔ court ↔ future ↔ executive
```

That would be **7 democracy**.

But the transition would be extremely dangerous:

```text
badly built:

population majority
        ↓
China/India dominance
        ↓
smaller states rebel
        ↓
disintegration or centralization
```

```text
properly built:

popular majority
+ states' majority
+ demoi protection
+ fundamental rights
+ regional autonomy
+ direct correction
        ↓
continental rosette-democracy
```

## 8. The most likely outcome

If introduced tomorrow:

```text
1. authoritarian members refuse genuinely free votes
2. democratic members argue over legitimacy
3. China and India are feared as demographic dominance poles
4. smaller members demand veto rights
5. the system blocks itself
6. BRICS falls apart or becomes a fake direct democracy
```

So in the short term:

> **It would tear BRICS apart rather than democratize it.**

But if built cleanly over decades, with rule of law, federalism, multiple majorities, and real media freedom, it would lead to something extremely powerful:

> **a direct-democratic mega-confederation of the Global South.**

It would not be a larger Switzerland. It would be closer to:

```text
Switzerland = direct democracy at Alpine scale

BRICS-Switzerland = direct democracy as a planetary pre-state
```

## Final formula

```text
Without rule of law:
BRICS direct democracy = plebiscitary façade

Without states' majority:
BRICS direct democracy = India/China dominance

Without minority protection:
BRICS direct democracy = tyranny of the majority

Without free media:
BRICS direct democracy = propaganda machine

With everything together:
BRICS direct democracy = 7-democracy as mega-confederation
```

The most precise sentence:

> **If BRICS were governed directly democratically like Switzerland, BRICS would either stop being BRICS — or stop being able to be authoritarian.**
