# Heptagonal Spiral Typology of Forms of Government

Yes — **this can be modeled very well**, but the cleanest shape is **not simply a heptagon**. It is closer to a **heptagonal spiral**, or even a **helix above a heptagon**.

Because you are really trying to express **two things at the same time**:

1. **Angle / position on the circle**  
   = how strongly power is concentrated or distributed

2. **Version 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0**  
   = historical / technological / institutional stage of development

A simple circle can only express (1).  
A simple timeline can only express (2).  
**Your model needs both.**

---

## 1. The right geometric form: heptagon + spiral height

So:

- **In the ground plan**: a **heptagon** with 7 forms of power
- **Vertically**: the stages 1.0, 2.0, 3.0
- **Together**: a **heptagonal helix / spiral**

So not:

```text
just a circle
```

but rather:

```text
Plane:      7 corners = 7 basic forms
Height:     1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0
Total:      7×3 = 21 states
```

Formally, one could say:

```text
Regime = (angular position in the heptagon, developmental stage)
```

or, more mathematically:

```text
R = C7 × L3
```

- `C7` = cycle graph with 7 positions
- `L3` = ordered ladder of versions 1, 2, 3

Even better:

```text
R = C7 × ℕ
```

if you later want to allow 4.0, 5.0, and so on.

---

## 2. But be careful: circle or spiral?

Here is a very important point.

When you say:

> from central concentration of power to broad distribution of power, like a clock hand that after 360 degrees starts again from the beginning

then there are **two possible interpretations**.

### A. Circle interpretation

After 360°, you are back at the same type.

This is useful if you want to say:

- history repeats itself
- every strong distribution eventually tips back into new concentration
- every order already contains its opposite

### B. Spiral interpretation

After 360°, you do **not** return to the same point, but emerge **above** the old point.

This is better if you want to say:

- dictatorship 2.0 is not the same as dictatorship 1.0
- democracy 3.0 is not the same as democracy 1.0
- there is recurrence, but at a new level

**I consider the spiral interpretation much stronger.**

So:

- **0°–360°** = version 1.0
- **360°–720°** = version 2.0
- **720°–1080°** = version 3.0

Then the same angle always represents the same basic logic, but at a different developmental stage.

---

## 3. What would the 7 points of the heptagon be?

If one pole is **maximum concentration of power** and the opposite region is **maximum distribution of power**, the 7 corners could be arranged as follows:

## Heptagon of power distribution

### Corner 1 — Monocracy

Maximum concentration in **one person / one center**

Examples:

- absolute monarchy
- dictatorship
- personalist rule

### Corner 2 — Cadre rule

Concentration in a **narrow power core**

Examples:

- junta
- politburo-style party leadership
- court clique

### Corner 3 — Oligarchy

Rule by a few dominant groups

Examples:

- aristocratic oligarchy
- economic oligarchy
- family rule

### Corner 4 — Mixed constitution / guided republic

There are several institutions, but power is not yet broadly distributed

Examples:

- early republic
- guided constitutionalism
- guided pluralism

### Corner 5 — Representative democracy

Power is more broadly distributed, but still bundled through representation

Examples:

- parliamentary democracy
- presidential democracy
- constitutional liberal state

### Corner 6 — Federal-pluralist order

Power is more strongly distributed spatially and institutionally

Examples:

- federation
- strong regionalism
- separation of powers + strong intermediate levels

### Corner 7 — Polycentric / network rule

Very broad distribution of power, many nodes, high subsidiarity

Examples:

- strong local autonomy
- direct / liquid / network-like democracy
- polycentric governance

Then it returns to corner 1 — but one stage higher:
not dictatorship 1.0 anymore, but dictatorship 2.0 or 3.0.

---

## 4. What do 1.0, 2.0, 3.0 mean at all?

That is the real core question.

The numbers should **not merely mean “better” or “worse.”**  
They should express the **form of social organization**.

The best meaning is:

- **1.0 = premodern / direct / personal**
- **2.0 = modern / bureaucratic / mass-societal**
- **3.0 = networked / digital / cybernetic / data-based**

So not moral, but **structural**.

---

## 5. The general meaning of 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0

### 1.0 — personal form of rule

Features:

- low institutional complexity
- power is attached to persons
- loyalty is personal
- information flows slowly
- low administrative density
- legitimacy: tradition, religion, ancestry, immediate force

As a formula:

```text
1.0 = Person > Institution
```

### 2.0 — institutional-bureaucratic form of rule

Features:

- organized administration
- mass media
- parties, bureaucracies, apparatuses
- nation-statehood
- large standardized systems
- legitimacy: constitution, ideology, mass mobilization, procedure

As a formula:

```text
2.0 = Institution > Person
```

### 3.0 — networked-cybernetic form of rule

Features:

- digital infrastructure
- real-time data
- platforms, algorithms, AI support
- polycentric and at the same time potentially hyper-centralized coordination
- strong feedback loops
- legitimacy: transparency, performance, feedback, participation, data trust

As a formula:

```text
3.0 = Network / Data / Feedback > classical institution
```

Or more precisely:

```text
3.0 = Institution + Network + algorithmic steering
```

---

## 6. Dictatorship 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0

Now it becomes concrete.

### Dictatorship 1.0

**Personal dictatorship**

Features:

- one leader, king, tyrant, warlord
- direct violence
- patrimonialism
- court, clan, army as instruments of power
- little fine-grained control of the population

Examples:

- ancient tyranny
- extreme forms of absolute princely rule
- caudillo systems
- tribal or military rule with a personal center

Formula:

```text
D1 = Violence + Person + loyalty network
```

---

### Dictatorship 2.0

**Bureaucratic-ideological dictatorship**

Features:

- not just leader, but apparatus
- party, secret police, administration, propaganda
- mass organization
- industrial surveillance
- high standardization
- ideology as a steering mechanism

Examples:

- totalitarian one-party states
- strongly bureaucratized military regimes
- centralized cadre states

Formula:

```text
D2 = Violence + Apparatus + Ideology + mass steering
```

---

### Dictatorship 3.0

**Cybernetic / data-based dictatorship**

Features:

- digital identities
- platform control
- algorithmic censorship
- behavioral prediction
- preventive steering
- nudging + surveillance + social scoring mechanisms
- concentration through data centers rather than only ministries

Not just “the state sees everything,” but:
**the system anticipates behavior**.

Formula:

```text
D3 = Data + Prediction + platform power + preventive steering
```

Key difference from D2:

- D2 controls visibly
- D3 often controls invisibly, continuously, automatically

---

## 7. Democracy 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0

### Democracy 1.0

**Early representative or proto-democratic form**

Features:

- limited participation
- early parliaments / assemblies
- slow communication
- elites still play a large role
- often only part of the population has the vote

Formula:

```text
Dem1 = Representation + limited participation
```

Examples:

- early republics
- early forms of parliamentarism
- constitutional systems with restricted suffrage

---

### Democracy 2.0

**Mass democracy of the industrial and welfare state**

Features:

- universal suffrage
- party competition
- mass media
- welfare state
- administrative state
- stable constitutional institutions

Formula:

```text
Dem2 = universal participation + representation + rule of law + mass organization
```

This is the classic modern democracy of the 20th century.

---

### Democracy 3.0

**Networked, transparent, multilevel democracy**

Features:

- digital participation
- citizens’ assemblies / citizens’ councils
- transparent data
- direct and representative elements combined
- federal and local coupling
- possible use of verified digital voting
- continuous feedback instead of only elections every few years
- stronger control of power through open information architecture

Formula:

```text
Dem3 = Representation + direct participation + digital transparency + permanent feedback
```

The core is:
**not the abolition of representation**, but its supplementation through better feedback.

---

## 8. Deeper still: 1.0 / 2.0 / 3.0 apply to all 7 corners

Actually, not only dictatorship and democracy, but **each of the 7 corners** can have 1.0, 2.0, and 3.0 versions.

Example: oligarchy.

### Oligarchy 1.0

- nobility
- families
- landed property
- court circles

### Oligarchy 2.0

- party elites
- industrial and financial elites
- state-adjacent cartels

### Oligarchy 3.0

- platform oligarchy
- data oligarchy
- technocratic-financial networks
- AI and infrastructure controllers

The same applies to federation, confederation, republic, and so on.

---

## 9. Why this can become cyclical

Your circle idea is strong because it captures the following:

## Every form produces its own problem

- **too much centralization** → rigidity, abuse, blindness
- **too much distribution** → slowness, fragmentation, veto players, coordination crises

Then often follows:

- too much concentration → opening
- too much distribution → new bundling

That is why this movement is plausible:

```text
Center → elite core → oligarchy → mixed form → representation → federalization → network distribution
→ then, because of coordination crisis, new centralization
```

But the new centralization is not the same as before.  
That is why it is a spiral, not a mere circle.

## 13. The concise definition

You could call the model:

## “Heptagonal Spiral Typology of Forms of Government”

**Definition:**

- the **angular position** describes the degree of power concentration or power distribution,
- the **rotation level** describes the civilizational stage of the same basic logic.

Short form:

```text
Angle = Who carries power?
Level = At what organizational level does it happen?
```

Or even shorter:

```text
Axis 1: Concentration ↔ Distribution
Axis 2: 1.0 ↔ 2.0 ↔ 3.0
```

## 15. Reading the versions as political evolution

The versions are not a moral ranking. A 3.0 dictatorship is not “more advanced” in the normative sense, but technically and organizationally more developed. Likewise, democracy 3.0 is not automatically better than democracy 2.0; it may be more transparent, participatory, and responsive, but it can also create new attack surfaces: manipulation, information overload, platform dependence, algorithmic bias.

The correct reading is therefore:

```text
1.0 = rule through person, tradition, local loyalty
2.0 = rule through institution, bureaucracy, mass organization
3.0 = rule through network, data, platform, feedback
```

---

## 16. The spiral logic in one sentence

History does not simply run from dictatorship to democracy or from centralization to distribution. It moves more like a rhythm between concentration and distribution, but at every new technical and institutional level, new forms of the same basic problems emerge.

Therefore, the compact formula is:

```text
Governmental development = recurrence of political basic logics at a higher organizational stage
```

Or sharper:

```text
The form returns, but the medium of power changes.
```
