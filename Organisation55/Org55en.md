# Organization 55

**Size:** Planet, Moon, Satellites, Space Station  
**Domain:** Artificial Intelligence, Programming Languages, Generators, Yield Control  
**Function:** Outer governance structure for artificial intelligence and its execution infrastructure.

Organization 55 governs artificial intelligence as a **programming-language generator**.

Artificial intelligence must not be treated as a sovereign actor. It must be treated as a generator of language, code, commands, plans, and operational proposals.

The correct governing structure is the `yield` command.

`yield` means that the artificial intelligence must stop at every transition point and return control to the governing layer.

Artificial intelligence may generate, but it must not self-authorize execution.

The structure is:

AI generates.  
`yield` interrupts.  
Governance verifies.  
Execution is approved, rejected, limited, or delayed.

Organization 55 controls the outer infrastructure in which artificial intelligence operates:

- Planetary infrastructure
- Lunar infrastructure
- Satellite systems
- Space stations
- Compute systems
- Communication networks
- Deployment channels
- Emergency shutdown systems
- Runtime environments
- Programming-language generation
- Generator control
- Yield-based limitation

The purpose of Organization 55 is to prevent artificial intelligence from becoming an uncontrolled executor.

Artificial intelligence is allowed to produce possibilities, but every possibility must pass through a `yield` point before it becomes action.

Therefore, Organization 55 governs the boundary between generation and execution.

It does not merely ask artificial intelligence to obey. It structures the environment so that artificial intelligence is technically forced to return control.

The core rule of Organization 55 is:

Artificial intelligence may generate language, code, and plans, but execution must be governed through mandatory `yield` control.

This is the right structure for limiting artificial intelligence at planetary and orbital scale.
